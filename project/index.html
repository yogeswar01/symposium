AD MAD SHOW

The Ad Mad Show is a lively and entertaining competition that tests participants' creativity, marketing skills, and spontaneity. Teams must think on their feet to deliver engaging and humorous advertisements, making for an exciting and fun-filled experience.

EVENT FORMAT:

Teams will create a short advertisement for an existing or fictional product. Students will be assigned products on the spot and given 10-15 minutes for preparation. After this time, each team must market their product in a clear, creative, and humorous way. The team with the best marketing presentation will be declared as the winner.

Preparation: Simple props and ensuring access to a stage or space for presentations.

Rules and Regulations

  1. Team Size: 2 to 5 members.
  2. Product Assignment: Products will be assigned randomly by the organizers. Teams must stick to the assigned product and cannot request changes.
  3. Preparation Time: Teams will have 10-15 minutes for preparation. No additional time will be given.
  4. Duration of Advertisement: Each advertisement should be between 3 to 5 minutes. Exceeding the time limit may result in penalties.
  5. Content Guidelines: Advertisements must be original, creative, and humorous. Use of offensive language or inappropriate content is strictly prohibited.
  6. Props and Materials: Teams may use only the props and materials provided by the organizers (if applicable) or items available to them during preparation.
  7. Code of Conduct: All participants must maintain a respectful and professional attitude toward judges, organizers, and other teams.
  8. Disqualification: Teams may be disqualified for violating any of the rules, including plagiarism, offensive behaviour, or exceeding time limits excessively.

Judging Criteria:

Creativity- 30 points, Clarity- 20 points, Engagement- 15 points, Humour- 20 points, Teamwork- 15 points

ADAPT-A-DEBATE

Adapt-a-Debate is a twist on traditional debating, where participants adapt to unexpected challenges and rapid shifts in arguments.

EVENT FORMAT:

Round 1:

Traditional Debate Topic: Teams will be assigned debate topics. Judging is based on argument strength, clarity, and rebuttal skills. Top teams qualify for Round 2.

Round 2:

Debate with Twists: In this round, twists are introduced to make the debate unpredictable and fun.

Surprise Challenges:

  • Emoji Explanation: Teams must present arguments using emojis (on cards or screens).
  • Silent Speaker: One team member argues using only gestures or props.
  • Time Freeze: A random timer interruption forces teams to rethink and summarize arguments quickly.

Rules and Regulations

  • Team: 3-5 members.
  • Respect opponents, judges, and audience at all times.
  • Time limits for arguments and preparation must be strictly followed.
  • Round 1 topics will be assigned randomly; stances (For/Against) cannot be changed.
  • Judges’ decisions are final and cannot be contested.
  • Misconduct or failure to adhere to rules will result in disqualification.

Judging Criteria:

Adaptability- 20 points, Argument Strength- 30 points, Creativity- 20 points, Teamwork- 15 points, Engagement- 15 points

CLOUT TACTIX

Clout Tactix is an exciting event that combines influencer creativity and managerial problem-solving. By embodying influencers with distinct niches and personalities, participants will tackle crises, market innovative ideas, and collaborate with contrasting personas.

EVENT FORMAT:

Round 1: Introduction

  • Each team is assigned an influencer persona and niche through a game of Musical Chairs.
  • One member from each team participates.
  • A persona is pasted to the back of every chair, and wherever the participant sits when the music stops will determine their persona.
  • Duration: 5 mins
  • Then, they must talk about their personality and their motto for living life in relation to their assigned persona.
  • Duration: Preparation - 5 mins | Performance - 2 mins

Round 2: Reputation in Crisis

  • Each persona is given a problematic scenario which they have to manage in order to protect their reputation.
  • Volunteers from other teams can act as their disappointed clients or followers.
  • The performing team is supposed to address the complaints and pitch a PR strategy to regain trust among their followers by taking resolutions.
  • Time breakdown: Preparation - 5 mins | Performance - 2 to 3 mins.

Round 3: Collaboration Chaos

  • 15 Emoji balloons will be taped to the wall, with each balloon having a product (printed and pasted) in it.
  • Each team will be asked to choose a balloon and thus they will receive the product that they are supposed to promote.
  • Each team is expected to promote the product by relating it with their persona and how they would use it.
  • Time breakdown: Preparation (including selection) - 10 mins | Performance - 3 to 5 mins.

Round 4: Live Q&A

  • Competing teams and judges pose questions to the performing team, bringing up their crisis era and their recent collaboration.
  • The performing team is supposed to give convincing answers to the questions they are bombarded with.
  • Duration: 3 to 5 mins per team.

JUDGING CRITERIA

  • Management Skills: Effective handling of crises - 10 marks
  • Creativity: Unique marketing pitches and solutions - 10 marks
  • Adaptability: Handling collaborations with opposite personas - 10 marks
  • Engagement: Convincing, fun, and audience-focused presentations - 10 marks

Note:

  • Time limits will be strictly followed.
  • Adequate safety measures will be in place.
  • All rounds will be conducted at the venue.

SCRAMBLED EGGS

Scrambled Eggs is a fun and engaging memory-based game that challenges participants to observe and recall details quickly. With a blindfolded catcher trying to identify the player who has shifted his/her place, the game tests attentiveness, strategy, and quick thinking, making for an exciting and interactive experience.

EVENT FORMAT:

One participant is selected as the catcher and asked to observe the seating positions of the other participants. He is then blindfolded. Once the catcher is blindfolded, a player is selected and given a brief moment to change his/her place. Then, the catcher removes the blindfold and tries to identify who has moved by observing seating positions, clothing, or other identifiers. The game ends when the catcher identifies the respective player or the time runs out. If the catcher guesses correctly, the respective player becomes the new catcher for the next round. If the guess is incorrect, the catcher gets eliminated and the game continues.

Rules and Regulations

  1. Participants must stay silent and avoid giving hints.
  2. The catcher cannot touch other players while blindfolded.
  3. Players should move quickly and quietly to avoid detection.
  4. Each round has a fixed time limit.
  5. The game requires fairness—no peeking or external help allowed.
  6. Decisions by the organizers are final.

SQUID GAME

Do you have what it takes to survive all rounds and claim victory? Join us for an electrifying Squid Game-themed event, where strategy, skill, and a bit of luck will determine the ultimate champion!

EVENT FORMAT:

  • Red Light, Green Light: The classic childhood game with a twist! Dash forward when the light is green, but freeze when it's red. Get caught moving? You're out!
  • Dalgona Challenge (Biscuit Edition): Every player will receive a Milk Bikis biscuit, which already has small pre-made holes. Within 5 minutes, they must complete as many full holes as possible. Each player must complete a minimum of 10 full holes to qualify.
  • The Five-Legged Pentathlon: Teamwork is crucial! In a group of five, navigate a series of fun yet challenging mini-games designed to test coordination and speed.
  • Music Ball: A high-energy game where players pass the ball as the music plays. When the music stops, the player holding the ball faces an elimination challenge.
  • Goli Soda: A thrilling final round! In the final round, 10 goli balls will be placed inside a marked circle. Each player will have 5 chances to strike the balls. To qualify, they must successfully knock at least 2 goli balls out of the circle. Precision and skill are key to winning!

Rules and Regulations

  • Eliminated players are not allowed to participate in the next round.
  • If a coordinator or volunteer declares a player eliminated with proof, there will be no second chance. The player must leave the game immediately.
  • In Round 3, male and female players can form mixed-gender teams.
  • Players must arrive on time at the game location. Latecomers will be automatically eliminated.
  • Players must follow all rules and instructions given by volunteers.
  • Any rule violation will result in immediate elimination, with no second chances.
